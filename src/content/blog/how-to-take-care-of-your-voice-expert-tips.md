**1. Stay Hydrated **

Your vocal cords need moisture to work smoothly. Aim to drink at least 6 - 8 glasses of water a day. Avoid excessive caffeine, alcohol, and sugary drinks, as they can dry out your throat. Herbal teas (like chamomile or ginger) are a great alternative for soothing your voice.

2. Warm Up Before You Speak

Before a meeting, performance, or class, give your voice a few minutes to wake up. Try gentle humming, lip trills, or light vocal slides. These simple exercises can prevent strain and help you sound more confident and resonant.

**3. Avoid Straining Your Voice**

Raising your voice over background noise or speaking for hours can fatigue your vocal cords. If you need to project, learn efficient breath support techniques (we teach these at Activate Your Voice). Remember, volume comes from the body, not the throat.

**4. Breath and Posture Matter**

Good breathing is the foundation of a healthy voice. Stand tall, keep your shoulders relaxed, and breathe from your diaphragm. Proper posture not only improves airflow but also helps your voice sound fuller and more natural.

**5. Give Your Voice Time to Rest **

Your vocal cords are muscles, which means they need recovery. If your voice feels hoarse or fatigued, rest it. Avoid whispering (it can strain your voice more than gentle speaking) and let your body recharge.

**7. Soothe and Moisturise Your Throat**

Keep your throat comfortable by sipping warm (not hot) liquids and avoiding excessively dry air. Steam inhalation or a personal humidifier can help keep your vocal cords hydrated, especially during colder months.

**8. Eat Smart for Vocal Health**

Avoid heavy, spicy, or acidic foods right before speaking engagements or performances as they can trigger reflux or mucus buildup. Light, balanced meals and plenty of water are the best fuel for your voice.
